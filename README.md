## Summary 
- This project allowed us to further our use of AI agents alongside code to boost code productivity. The core concept students need to understand is how to be specific when asking co-pilot / their favorite LLMs like GPT,Gemini, or Claude when evaluating a project structure, and attributes in creation.
  
- Students may be most likely to struggle when prompting Co-pilot to evaluate certain features like class or template generations. 

- When in the pipeline of draft --> publish.mds, co-pilot likes to give unwanted suggestions that may come from comments you put out. Students may click on these comments immediately rather than taking the time to evaluate if the change is necessary before pushing.
 
- AI was helpful in providing basic templates that follow the schema and allow the work to necessarily be built off of. A way to guide our students without giving them the answer would be to ask them to tackle the plan (pseudo code) portion of our UPI.

-  This "planned" portion gives us an idea of what they're thinking, and examine their though process, before considering adding any final changes. Overall, this project engages students and fundamentally teaches them the importance of AI use in productivity and straying away from overreliance.
